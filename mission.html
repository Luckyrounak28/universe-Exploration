Space Missions

Explore the significant space missions conducted by various countries and research centers.

Apollo 11

Conducted by NASA, Apollo 11 was the first manned mission to land on the Moon on July 20, 1969. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the lunar surface.

Voyager

Launched by NASA in 1977, the Voyager missions (Voyager 1 and Voyager 2) explored the outer planets and are now in interstellar space, sending back valuable data about the outer reaches of our solar system.

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched in 1990 by NASA and ESA, the Hubble Space Telescope has provided some of the most detailed images of distant galaxies, nebulae, and other astronomical phenomena, revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

Curiosity Rover

Launched by NASA in 2011, the Curiosity Rover has been exploring the surface of Mars, conducting experiments to assess the planet's habitability and geological history.

Chandrayaan-2

Launched by ISRO in 2019, Chandrayaan-2 aimed to explore the Moon's south pole region. The mission included an orbiter, a lander (Vikram), and a rover (Pragyan).

Tianwen-1

Launched by CNSA in 2020, Tianwen-1 is China's first mission to Mars, consisting of an orbiter, a lander, and a rover (Zhurong) to study the Martian surface and atmosphere.

Rosetta

Launched by ESA in 2004, the Rosetta mission studied comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko, providing valuable data on the composition and behavior of comets.

Juno

Launched by NASA in 2011, the Juno mission is studying Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetic field, and gravitational field to understand the planet's formation and evolution.

Hayabusa2

Launched by JAXA in 2014, the Hayabusa2 mission studied asteroid Ryugu and successfully returned samples to Earth, providing insights into the early solar system.
